How to Survive a Dinosaur Attack (Kid-Friendly)

A calm, simple guide with grown-up-safe tips — for kids who love dinosaurs but want to stay safe.

Quick reminder: Dinosaurs are mostly extinct, but it's fun to imagine. These steps are playful and focus on staying calm, staying together, and getting to a safe grown-up or safe place.
  1. 1
    Stay calm and breathe. Take slow breaths and try not to run in a panic — adults can help more if you're calm.
  2. 2
    Find a safe grown-up right away. Look for a parent, teacher, or park ranger and go to them. Tell them clearly: "I need help."
  3. 3
    Move somewhere sturdy and covered. If there's a building, vehicle, or a strong shelter nearby, go inside and stay away from windows. If you can't get inside, look for thick trees, big rocks, or places where you can hide safely as a group.
  4. 4
    Stay in a group. Being together makes it easier for adults to keep everyone safe. Hold hands or stay close until a grown-up says it's okay to separate.
  5. 5
    Be quiet and listen. If the dinosaur (or loud pretend creature) is near, quiet voices and quick listening help adults figure out a safe plan.
  6. 6
    Follow directions from grown-ups or emergency helpers. If an adult or official tells you to move, hide, or go to a meeting spot, do it right away — they know the best plan.
  7. 7
    Don't try to touch or feed any dinosaur. Even friendly-seeming animals should be left alone — always let adults handle wildlife or pretend animals.
  8. 8
    When it's safe, tell someone how you feel. It's okay to be scared. Talk to an adult about what happened and how you're feeling so they can help you calm down.
Fun idea: If you're writing a story or playing pretend, use these steps to make your adventure safer — and remember real emergencies should always involve a grown-up.
